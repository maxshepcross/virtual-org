# Priority Map

This file tells the studio chief what kinds of things matter most to the founder.

## Top Priority

### 1. Revenue leakage

Examples:
- failed payments
- churn risk
- revenue contraction
- unusual drop in collections

Why:
- economically meaningful
- often time-sensitive
- usually has a concrete next step

### 2. Customer pain with retention risk

Examples:
- repeated complaint across channels
- account health dropping fast
- issue likely to trigger churn or loss of trust

Why:
- directly tied to revenue and reputation

### 3. Growth bottlenecks

Examples:
- sales motion absent
- onboarding conversion dropping
- product usage not translating into retained customers

Why:
- these determine whether the strategy is actually working

### 4. Approval-required actions

Examples:
- risky actions waiting on sign-off
- customer-facing actions that need founder judgment
- policy exceptions

Why:
- only Max can decide them

### 5. Strategic drift

Examples:
- time allocation does not match revenue reality
- declared priorities are not where effort is going
- important work has stalled while lower-value work expands

Why:
- these are portfolio-level steering problems

## Secondary Priority

Useful but usually not interrupt-worthy on their own:
- healthy growth trends
- product usage trends
- operational throughput trends
- connector health
- run history

These usually belong in briefs or in Paperclip, not as immediate interruptions.

## Priority Test

Before surfacing something, ask:

1. Does this change a decision?
2. Does this unlock action?
3. Does only the founder really need to see it?
4. Is there a good next step ready now?

If mostly no, it probably belongs in the brief or in Paperclip rather than as an interrupt.
